# fleek-hackathon

## Fleek x a16z Hackathon Context

### AI in Supply Chain Strategy

#### Company Overview and Core Problems
- Secondhand/recommerce platform operating internationally for ~5 years.
- Three core challenges driving the product:
  1. Getting inventory listed and online (currently manual, labor-intensive).
  2. Matching buyers to unique secondhand items (every item is one-of-a-kind).
  3. Building trust for high-value cross-border transactions.

#### AI Strategy: Three Pillars
1. AI as force multiplier for internal teams (“clockwork on steroids”).
2. AI-powered matching and discovery for buyers and sellers.
3. Custom AI model development (Heritage) for item identification and categorization.

- Frontier models were ruled out: too slow (10-15s per item) and too costly (~$6M/year at scale).
- Current stack is built on open-source vision models, fine-tuned on proprietary data.
- Accuracy, speed, and cost all improved after switching approaches.
- System has been live in production for 6-8 months.

#### Data and Pricing Engine
- 5+ years of transaction data:
  - 10M+ items sold.
  - 100M+ items listed.
- Pricing engine uses historical comparables to estimate clearing price for unique items.
- Quality control centers in each operating country check every item before shipping.

#### Hackathon Format and Judging
- Three top teams per track advance to a grand final.
- Grand final includes a live demo on stage, judge deliberation, and prizes for 1st/2nd/3rd.
- Judging criteria:
  - Working demo (not mockups or design-only).
  - Solves a real problem.
  - Technical impressiveness.
  - Ambition of the idea.
- Judges include Megan Moore (partner at Novice) and others TBC.
- Teams can only compete in one track.
